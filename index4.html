Read/Write learning style



Students who excel at traditional study methods are often read/write learners. They're the type of students who don't mind sitting down and reading a textbook in order to learn something new. Learners who can read and write do well in lectures because they prefer to take careful notes on what the teacher says. Their note-taking is a strength of theirs because it enhances their learning and later serves as a useful study aid.




Read/write learners are often independent, but they can expand their intellectual horizons by being exposed to new study and job methods that are outside of their comfort zone. Many students are unaware of their learning style, therefore the teacher's responsibility in their lives is to help them discover it and build on their strengths.


  • A textbook or reading is the greatest way for them to learn.
  • They're excellent note-takers.
  • Enjoys writing essays, reports, and other similar materials.
  • Uses a dictionary, thesaurus, or handbook to learn.
  • Words are the key to this learner's heart.



Things to do that will help with learning


  1. Taking thorough notes during lectures to be used later to read, rewrite, or condense.
  2. Studying in a quiet environment void of distractions
  3. Make charts and graphs into a story or a statement
  4. Read from the textbook for clarification
  5. Make use of handouts, worksheets and study guides
