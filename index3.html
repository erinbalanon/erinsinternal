Aural learning style



Auditory learners may appear to be less attentive in the classroom since they prefer to absorb information while the teacher speaks rather than taking notes. For some students, writing is a distraction from fully immersing themselves in a lecture, yet for others, writing is one of the most effective forms of reinforcement. These students are also more talkative than their peers and may ask more questions during lectures to reinforce learning through hearing instances. Patience is a virtue that these students must have.




In class, auditory learners tend to listen, talk, memorise, and debate. They prefer audiobooks to print books for learning.


  • Learn by hearing and listening
  • You understand and remember things you have heard
  • You store information by the way it sounds
  • Easier time understanding spoken instructions
  • Often learn by reading out loud because you have to hear it or speak it in order to know it



Things to do that will help with learning


  1. Sit where you can hear
  2. Hearing checks regularly
  3. Flashcards to learn new words; read them out loud
  4. Read stories/assignments/directions out loud
  5. Record yourself spelling or reading words
  6. Have test questions read out to you
  7. Study new material by reading it out loud
