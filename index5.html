Kinesthetic learning style



You may be aware that kinesthetic learners are hands-on learners who need to walk through a process to fully comprehend its dynamics. Kinesthetic learners are uncommon; according to studies, just 5% of students learn best through direct hands-on experiences. Kinesthetic learners are often referred to as tactile learners. There is, however, a distinction to be made between the two. The kinesthetic learner isn't limited to learning through touch; instead, they use sensory input from all five senses to better understand what they're doing. Kinesthetic learners learn life skills more rapidly than others, such as cooking, which allows for a scenario in which all five senses are involved.




These students enjoy doing hands-on projects like taking things apart and reassembling them, playing with Legos, or completing a puzzle. Kinesthetic learners find it more difficult to adjust to a traditional classroom environment.


  • Physical rewards are appealing to them.
  • Most of the time, they're on the move.
  • When speaking, they like to touch people.
  • When studying, they tap their pencil or their foot.
  • They enjoy participating in activities.
  • Reading isn't a top priority for them.
  • Problems are best solved by physically working through them.
  • Will experiment with new things.
  • By nature, they are outgoing and use physical expression to express their emotions.
  • When speaking, they use their hands.



Things to do that will help with learning


  1. Work standing up
  2. Use small movements to help focus such as bouncing a ball
  3. Exercise between study breaks
  4. Use highlighters and flashcards
  5. Use creative methods to study such as creating mindmaps, making a storyboard or making a short video
  6. Act out ideas and information
  7. Practice tasks repeatedly
  8. Watch videos
